› so, in this single well case, we have a single well that may thermilize by third body collsions and it may dissociate into some other product. Do we solve with the
  steady state solver the master eqiation? and do we iterate the soltution to get a convergent flux for the source term?


• Yes, for the single-well chemical-activation steady-state modes, you solve a steady-state master equation of the form:

  J * N_ss = R_form * F

  where:

  - J contains collision + reactive loss terms,
  - F is a normalized source shape,
  - R_form (formation flux) is fixed input.

  But no, you do not do DISLIT-style self-consistent source iteration in this single-well path.

  - Source is fixed during the solve.
  - You solve once per condition.
  - In one kernel variant (ExponentialBandedMess), the linear algebra solver may iterate (BiCGSTAB/Gauss-Seidel), but that is only numerical iteration for N_ss, not
    source-flux repopulation.

  Also, in ThermalDissociation mode, it does not solve the steady-state source-driven equation; it computes canonical averaging directly.
